01What Scoutsi Is
What is Scoutsi?+

Scoutsi is an intelligent decision system for the automotive value chain. It maintains a continuously updated model of an organisation's suppliers, partners, dependencies, and exposures — commercial, operational, and regulatory — and reasons over that model in real time, so that when the chain shifts, the business knows what it means before the window to act has closed.

Reports, disclosures, and compliance outputs are produced by the system as a natural by-product. They are not its purpose. The purpose is decision-ready intelligence, at the moment decisions need to be made.

Is Scoutsi a software platform or a consulting firm?+

Scoutsi is neither a conventional software platform nor a consulting firm.

Scoutsi combines a sector-specific intelligent decision platform with a structured approach to embedding it inside your organisation — so capability stays with you, not with an external team. The goal is to make your people faster and better at consequential decisions, not to create a dependency on advisors or annual contracts.

Why automotive specifically?+

The automotive value chain is one of the most complex decision environments in any industry. Multi-tier supply chains spanning dozens of countries. OEM-driven data cascades. Rapid regulatory change across multiple jurisdictions. Tariff and geopolitical exposure that forces decisions to be made overnight.

Most enterprise intelligence tools are built for simpler environments and adapted — poorly — for automotive. Scoutsi is built from the ground up for this sector's specific structure, its entity types, its data flows, and the decisions its leaders actually have to make.

That is why automotive is where the architecture is built first — and why solving it here means it generalises.

What makes Scoutsi different?+

Most AI tools reason over scraped consensus data and hope the answer holds up. Scoutsi:

  • Starts from primary data,
  • Applies sector intelligence built specifically for the automotive value chain, and
  • Derives answers rather than generating them — so every output carries its own evidence trail, from source to conclusion.

Built bottom-up for defensibility. Not top-down for demos.

What problem does Scoutsi fundamentally solve?+

Automotive organisations are not failing to collect data. They are failing to connect it — to each other, to decisions, and to the moment those decisions need to be made.

Value chains now change faster than annual planning cycles can track. A tariff shifts, a supplier fails, a regulation takes effect — and the commercial, operational, and regulatory consequences ripple through the business before anyone has had time to model them. By the time a report lands, the window to act has often already closed.

Scoutsi closes that gap. It gives the right intelligence to the right person before the decision window closes — not as a dashboard to browse, but as structured, traceable, actionable intelligence surfaced at the moment it is needed.

02The Platform
How does Scoutsi work at a high level?+

Scoutsi builds and maintains a living model of your value chain — every supplier, partner, and dependency; every commercial, operational, and regulatory exposure it carries. That model is updated continuously as conditions change, not annually when a report is due.

The system reasons over this model to surface decision intelligence: costed scenarios when a shock hits, watchpoints when conditions are shifting, and structured outputs — compliance reports, disclosure-ready reports, OEM data responses — produced as natural by-products of a system that already holds the underlying data.

  • A living value chain model — current as of today, not last year
  • Continuous reasoning over changing conditions, not periodic snapshots
  • Decision intelligence surfaced when it is needed, not retrieved on request
  • Compliance and reporting outputs generated as by-products
Does Scoutsi replace our ERP, EHS, or existing systems?+

No. Scoutsi is designed to sit alongside what you already run — SAP, ERPs, data warehouses, existing ESG tools. It connects and reasons over data those systems already hold. You keep your systems, your data, and your control. Scoutsi adds the intelligence layer that turns that data into decisions.

How is our data protected? Does it leave our environment?+

Data handling differs by product, by design.

Enterprise
Scoutsi Enterprise is deployed within your own infrastructure — your network, your cloud; not on Scoutsi's servers. Your data never enters Scoutsi's systems. Data sovereignty is guaranteed by architecture, not by policy.
Snapshot
Scoutsi Snapshot runs on Scoutsi's cloud infrastructure. Your data is stored in an isolated account, and you control what you share and with whom. Nothing is shared without your explicit consent.
Are we locked into a proprietary data model or vendor ecosystem?+

No. You retain full ownership of your data, full control over its structure and access, and full independence from proprietary lock-ins. Scoutsi is system-agnostic and built to integrate, not to capture.

How is intellectual property handled?+

Your IP is yours. Any data, models, or outputs generated from your data remain your property.

Where Scoutsi's own IP is deployed — the sector intelligence, the reasoning architecture, the platform itself — this is governed by a standard licensing agreement that permits full usage within your organisation while protecting Scoutsi's proprietary methods.

Scoutsi gives the CEO line of sight into those consequences — across the full value chain, continuously, not just at annual review. It converts sustainability and value-chain exposure from a delegated reporting topic into governed enterprise intelligence.

04Enterprise & Snapshot — One System, Two Entry Points
Who is Scoutsi Enterprise for?+

Scoutsi Enterprise is for OEMs and Tier-1 suppliers facing the full complexity of the value chain — Scope 3 obligations, CSRD and ESRS reporting, OEM data cascade requirements, and the board-level pressure to govern all of it with financial-grade rigour.

It is deployed within your own environment. Your data never leaves your walls. Every output is traceable from source to conclusion. It is built for the moment a decision has to survive an audit — not just for demos.

Who is Scoutsi Snapshot for?+

Scoutsi Snapshot is for MSMEs and Tier-2/3 suppliers facing a specific, urgent pressure: OEM customers operating under CSRD, ESRS, and OEM cascade requirements are increasingly required to account for supply chain emissions — and that obligation is landing on smaller suppliers as data requests and contract conditions.

Snapshot produces a credible, screening-grade answer in days: a traceable product carbon footprint from sector intelligence and your own data — without a sustainability team, without clean data to start, without a six-month project. Your data stays isolated to your own account, and you decide what to share.

Are Enterprise and Snapshot different products?+

They share the same underlying system — the same sector intelligence, the same reasoning architecture, the same commitment to primary data and explicit lineage. What differs is the entry point and deployment model.

Enterprise is a deep, per-customer deployment within your environment. Snapshot is a faster, lighter entry point designed for the MSME reality — lower cost, lower internal resource requirement, and a path to upgrade as needs evolve.

05Getting Started
Where do I start — Enterprise or Snapshot?+

It depends on where you sit in the value chain and what you need to solve first.

If you are an OEM or Tier-1 supplier managing Scope 3 obligations, board-level reporting, or supplier cascade requirements across a complex chain, Scoutsi Enterprise is the right conversation. It is a bespoke deployment — we engage selectively and scope carefully before committing to a POC.

If you are an MSME or Tier-2/3 supplier facing an OEM data request, a PCF deadline, or a compliance obligation you cannot fund a full enterprise engagement to address, Scoutsi Snapshot is designed for you. You can be up and running in days, without a consultant and without a project.

How do I get started with Scoutsi Snapshot?+

Snapshot is self-serve. Sign up, connect your data, and Scoutsi generates a screening-grade, confidence-tiered product carbon footprint — typically within days. No consultant. No six-month implementation. No clean data required to begin.

Your outputs are transparently labelled: every figure shows what is derived from primary data, what is estimated from sector intelligence, and where contributing more of your own data would improve confidence. You start with what you have and the picture strengthens over time.

How do I get started with Scoutsi Enterprise?+

Enterprise begins with a conversation, not a contract. We work with a select number of organisations where we believe the fit is real and the impact will be significant — so the first step is a strategic discussion about your value-chain exposures and where decision latency is costing you most.

If there is a fit, we scope a paid POC: tightly bounded, focused on one priority use case, and designed to deliver a working slice of your decision backbone in 8–12 weeks. The POC is the foundation for full deployment — not a separate exercise.

What does a POC involve?+

A paid, tightly scoped proof of concept focused on one priority use case — typically one where the value of faster, more defensible intelligence is immediately visible.

It is designed to be low-risk and proportionate: a short working session to map how data currently flows through your business, a defined scope agreed upfront, and 8–12 weeks to a live, working slice that your people can actually use.

  • One focal point from your team — ideally from the function with highest urgency
  • Limited subject matter expert input (a few hours per week)
  • Access to existing reports and data — can be obfuscated where necessary
Why is the POC paid?+

Because it delivers something real. The POC produces a working tool scoped to your use case — not a demo, not a slide deck. It is priced to be proportionate to the value it creates, and there is an option to move directly to full deployment once the POC is complete.

What is the first meeting actually for?+

It is not a pitch. It is a strategic conversation — about your value-chain exposures, where the decision latency is costing you most, and whether Scoutsi is the right fit for your specific situation.

We will share what leading automotive organisations are preparing for and where the highest-leverage opportunities typically lie. You will leave with a clearer picture of your own priorities, whether or not we proceed.

What happens after the POC?+

When the POC delivers — and it is scoped precisely so that it does — the path to full deployment is a short step, not a new procurement cycle. The working tool produced in the POC is the foundation.

We also continue to evolve the platform alongside you: deeper value-chain coverage, broader regulatory alignment, stronger supply chain integration — driven by real-world use cases, not product roadmap assumptions.
